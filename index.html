From: Pep Talk

Subject: Are you comparing yourself to success stories?
Mar 1

Hey there,

I got into this maker/founder game as a result of reading success stories. I saw people not too different from myself who decided to do things their way, and won.

It was inspiring, but success stories don't always mix nicely with that human instinct of judging by comparison. They get us automatically comparing ourself, and the result is a sinking feeling that "I'm not as good as them". Self-doubt. Have you been there too?

Of course, you already know that perspective is pure BS. It's born of survivorship bias, negativity bias, and other cognitive biases. Yet sometimes we accept it as truth, despite knowing better.

Here's a more useful perspective to replace it with:

If you could see the people behind those success stories before they made it big, you'd have a very different perspective. They'd probably look a lot like you do now: someone with big potential who hasn't quite cracked it yet.

They're that same successful person you felt inferior to, just in a different phase of their life.

They had to get through that phase to find their success, and so will you. And when you succeed, there'll be a bunch of newbies comparing themselves to your success story.
